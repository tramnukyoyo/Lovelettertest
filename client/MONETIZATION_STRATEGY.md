## Part 2: Ethical Monetization Research Findings

### What Works (2025 Best Practices)

**âœ… Proven Ethical Models:**
1. **Cosmetic-Only Purchases** - No gameplay advantage
   - Skins, avatars, themes, emotes
   - Most popular for ethical games
   - Player-friendly and sustainable

2. **Freemium with Clear Value** - Free core game, optional premium
   - Premium: Ad-free, extra features, cosmetics, cosmetic battle pass
   - Free users can still win competitive games
   - Example: Dota 2, League of Legends cosmetic model

3. **Battle Pass / Seasonal Premium** - Limited-time cosmetic progression
   - Paid tier: Exclusive cosmetics, themed rewards
   - Free tier: Also has progression (just slower)
   - Creates natural engagement cycles

4. **Supporter Tiers** - Optional "buy me coffee" style
   - Low price ($1-5/month)
   - No gameplay advantage
   - Shows you appreciate player support

5. **Educational Licensing** - For SchoolQuizGame specifically
   - Free for individual students
   - Paid tiers for school/institutional use
   - Fair market: $5-50/month per school

### What NOT to Do (âŒ Player-Damaging Models)

**âŒ AVOID:**
- âŒ **Pay-to-Win** - Purchasing better gameplay elements
- âŒ **Aggressive Monetization** - Constant prompts to spend
- âŒ **Exploitative Pricing** - $99.99 cosmetics (excessive)
- âŒ **Loot Boxes** - Gambling mechanics with real money
- âŒ **Forced Ads** - Annoying pop-ups between rounds
- âŒ **Bait & Switch** - Free promise then paywall
- âŒ **P2P Advantage** - "Rich players win more"
